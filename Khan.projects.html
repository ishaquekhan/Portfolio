Project: Entrepreneurship Project

Course: AP Computer Science A (2016-2017)

Type of Project: Group

This was our final major project of AP Computer Science A. Our task was to find a problem in today's world and create a solution. I came up with the idea of long range wireless charging which could be used with electric vehicles and/or hybrids. Current solutions do not provide a reasonable way of tackling this problem, as they try to use the same wireless charging technology as smartphones. In order for this solution to be viable, new wireless technology must be used. For this reason, I decided to use a wireless power transfer system involving lenses, laser designators, and photocell receptors. In short, this method utilizes high frequency electromagnetic waves that are refracted into beams than can be aimed at photocell receptors on individual vehicles thanks to the laser designators. Project: AOI Logic Design

Course: Digital Electronics (2017-2018)

Type of Project: Individual

This was the second major project of Digital Electronics. This project required me to think of a situation where AOI Logic could be implemented. I thought of the college admissions process, and the fact that a student may want some closure after applying to gain a rough estimate based on whether or not he or she got in. The circuit I designed had four inputs, test scores, grades, essays, and extracurricular activities, which are all crucial to the admissions process. The circuit will output an estimate of whether or not a particular student got in. A truth table was then developed and a logic expression was derived from it. Next, the logic expression was simplified and turned into a MultiSim file to simulate the proposed circuit. After verifying the circuit's functionality in MultiSim, it was breadboarded.
